    Frequently Asked Questions

    How do I negotiate salary in India?▼
    The most effective approach is to have a clear number in mind, cite your experience and market benchmarks, and frame the ask professionally. Be specific about your target CTC and explain why you deserve it. Always negotiate before accepting — most Indian employers expect it and 70%+ of offers have room to move.
    How much hike should I ask for when switching jobs in India?▼
    In India, a 20–30% hike is standard when switching companies in IT/software. Finance and consulting typically offer 15–25%. Marketing and operations roles see 15–20%. Anything below 15% when switching jobs is generally considered below market. Use the benchmark panel in this tool to see what's reasonable for your industry.
    Should I negotiate via email or phone in India?▼
    Phone or video call is preferred for senior roles (5+ years) as it lets you build rapport and read reactions. Email is safer for freshers or if you need time to compose your thoughts — it also gives a paper trail. This tool generates both formats.
    What if the company says the offer is final?▼
    Ask about other components: joining bonus, variable pay, ESOPs, earlier review cycle, or remote work flexibility. Even if base salary is fixed, these can significantly increase total compensation. Try: "I understand if the base is fixed — is there flexibility on the joining bonus or the first review timeline?"
    Will negotiating hurt my chances of getting the offer?▼
    Almost never. Companies in India expect candidates to negotiate — especially for mid-senior roles. A polite, well-reasoned counter-offer rarely results in rescinded offers. What matters is tone: be enthusiastic about the role while firm about the number. The scripts in this tool are specifically designed to be professional and non-confrontational.
